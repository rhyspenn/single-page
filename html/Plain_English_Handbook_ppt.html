Writing Principles (1/3)

Active Voice

Strong Verbs

Use subject-performs-action structure ("Investor buys stock"). Avoid passive voice ("Stock is bought") unless essential. Find hidden verbs in nouns.

Use Pronouns

You & We

Speak directly ("you," "we"). Clarifies roles, avoids abstraction, shortens sentences, bypasses gender issues.

Be Concrete

Avoid Abstractions

Replace abstract terms with concrete ones or use examples/scenarios. Go from general to specific (Asset -> Stock).

Cut Clutter

Concise Language

Eliminate superfluous words ("in order to" -> "to"). Avoid redundant phrasing or lists of synonyms.

Writing Principles (2/3)

Write Positively

Avoid Negatives

Positive sentences are clearer ("Only X..." vs "Not Y..."). Replace negative phrases with single positive words ("not certain" -> "uncertain").

Short Sentences

Break it Down

Avoid overly long, complex sentences. Break down information-packed sentences for better comprehension. Provide context first.

Drop Jargon

Use Common Words

Eliminate legal/financial jargon. Use short, common words. Explain necessary technical terms on first use. Avoid new acronyms.

Simple Words

Choose Simpler Synonyms

Use simpler synonyms when available ("end" not "terminate", "use" not "utilize").

Writing Principles (3/3)

Natural Order

Subject-Verb-Object

Keep subject, verb, object close together (SVO order). Avoid long interrupting modifiers.

Use If-Then

Conditionals

Structure conditional statements clearly. Handle multiple conditions logically, perhaps with tabulation or tables.

Parallelism

Consistent Structure

Ensure list items use parallel grammatical form (all nouns, all verbs) for clarity and rhythm.

Avoid "Respectively"

Clarity Over Brevity

Forces readers to backtrack. Rewrite sentences to state relationships directly and clearly.

Designing the Document

Thoughtful design enhances readability and understanding.

Hierarchy

Levels of Info

Use distinct styles (type, size, weight) for headings/text to show structure. Limit levels.

Typography

Typefaces & Size

Choose readable fonts (Serif for text, Sans-serif for headings). Use appropriate size (10-12pt+). Avoid all caps. Limit to 2 typefaces.

Layout

Spacing & Alignment

Use white space. Use left-justified, ragged-right text. Ensure good line spacing. Keep lines short (32-64 chars). Use columns, bullets, tables.

Graphics

Visual Data

Keep simple. Avoid "chartjunk" (e.g., fake 3D). Use correct scale/proportions. Be consistent. Show time flowing forward. Integrate with text. Use pie charts sparingly.

Color

Use Wisely

Balance black/white using type weights, shading, etc. Ensure good contrast if using color. Don't let design hinder legibility.

Trust Your Eye

Evaluate Critically

Ultimately, if a design seems unclear or unhelpful, it probably is, regardless of guidelines. Ensure honesty and efficiency.
